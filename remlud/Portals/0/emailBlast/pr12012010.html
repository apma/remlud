David Bargen Appointed to UNK Alumni Board

  

Attorney David J. A. Bargen has been elected to the University of Nebraska at Kearney Alumni Association Board of Directors.  He is one of nine members elected to serve a three-year term beginning with the Alumni Association Board’s fall meeting.  David will assist the alumni board in its mission of promoting communication and interaction among alumni, students, faculty, administrators and friends of UNK.

David graduated from UNK in 1996 with a BS in Political Science and Public Administration.  While attending UNK, he served as the Student Body President and Student Regent during his senior year, and was a member of the Student Senate and active in various student activities during his time at UNK.  David received the Distinguished Service Award for service on the University of Nebraska Board of Regents, received the William Nester Leadership Award, and the Outstanding Senior Award.  While attending UNK, David was also an intern with the City of Kearney City Manager’s Office.  After graduation, he worked as an academic advisor and college instructor in the Student Affairs Division of UNK while earning a Master’s of Public Administration degree from the University of Nebraska at Omaha.

After earning his MPA, David attended the University of Nebraska College of Law and was named an Executive Editor of the Nebraska Law Review, and was President of his law school class all three years.  He graduated in 2004 with High Distinction, ranked second in his law class.  David also was named to the Order of the Coif, was named a Theodore Sorenson Fellow, and earned the College of Law’s Outstanding Law Student Award.

David, an associate with Rembolt Ludtke LLP, concentrates his practice in the areas of municipal law, water law, and general litigation.  Prior to joining the firm, David clerked for the Honorable C. Arlen Beam, Judge on the United States Court of Appeals for the Eighth Circuit, from 2004 to 2006.  David was a member of Class II of the State Chamber’s Leadership Nebraska Program in 2008.  He is a member of the Nebraska Bar Association and Lincoln Bar Association.  He is currently First Vice President of the Board of Directors for the Lincoln Literacy Council and a member of the Nebraska Groundwater Foundation Advisory Board.
